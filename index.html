The Concept Car Blog

A Desired Concept?

Ronan Glon
Oct. 28, 2023

PInfiniti, known for its plans to release new models, is developing an electric sedan inspired by the Vision Qe concept. Contrary to industry trends moving away from sedans, Infiniti believes in the persistent demand for this vehicle type, particularly in the U.S. market. Bob Welby, Infiniti's senior director of operations, highlighted the brand's historical significance, passionate fan base, and ongoing interest in sedans.

The article mentioned earlier models like the G35 and G37, derived from Nissan Z, which prioritized performance over comfort, targeting European sport sedans like the BMW 3 Series. Even in 2023, these models maintain a dedicated following. However, with the approaching end of the Q50's life cycle, Infiniti faces a future without a sedan. This contrasts with their current lineup, which heavily features four SUVs, an area in which Infiniti has excelled in the past decade. Bob Welby emphasized that the focus on SUVs would continue, even if sedans persist. Furthermore, the impending arrival of the next-generation QX80, hinted at by the QX Monograph concept, reinforces the brand's commitment to SUV development and solidifies their strategy in that segment. Infiniti's dedication to SUV development is evident in their strategic focus.

Electric or Combustion Engine?

Ana Almerini
Oct. 06, 2023

Electric cars have gained popularity due to incentives and environmental awareness, yet weighing their pros and cons is crucial before transitioning. Advantages include lower running costs and reduced carbon emissions due to zero greenhouse gas production. They necessitate less maintenance and offer high performance and quieter operation. Cost-efficient charging further appeals to consumers, although savings vary by location.

Pros Cons
Lower Costs Fewer Charging Stations
Lower Carbon Footprint Long Charge Time
Less Maintenance Short Driving Range

However, downsides exist. Locating charging stations, particularly on rural journeys, remains a challenge even though they become more available. Charging time, even at the fastest stations, takes longer than refueling a gas car. Limited driving range and high costs, though offset by savings, are drawbacks. Additionally, replacing battery packs can be expensive. The decision to own an electric car hinges on individual needs and priorities. For short commutes and a focus on reducing fossil fuel dependence and costs, an electric vehicle may be suitable, especially when complemented by solar panels for an emissions-free lifestyle. Understanding these issues is crucial to determinenwhether an electric car aligns with a person's needs and environmental goals.
